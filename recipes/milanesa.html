Milanesa Napolitana Recipe

Description:

With a topping of melted cheese, tomato sauce and the scent of oregano, this meal can't be beaten.
Breaded-Round Steak (also know as Milanesa) is an Argentinian national dish, very easy to prepare in a few minutes for a complete meal that few can resist.
It is a gastronomic delicacy known for its adaptability, so among its many versions, it can be eaten "a la napolitana" (with tomato sauce and cheese), with fried egg, with ham or with lemon, and accompanied by mashed potatoes, salads or French fries.

Ingredients:

  • Many round steaks as you want
  • Salt and pepper
  • a cup of flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 17 oz of bread crumbs
  • a onion
  • a can of tomato
  • a couple slices of cheese
  • a couple slices of cooked ham

Preparation:

  1. Season the round steak with salt and papper. Coat them in flour, beaten egg, and breadcrumbs, pressing firmly to ensure a good breading.
  2. Fry them until it's golden on both sides and then set aside on absorbent paper.
  3. For the sauce, process a small onion with the tomato and oregano, season, add an bay leaf if you want, and then cook this mixture over low heat for 20 minutes.
  4. Finally, place it in a baking dish and top each one with a spoonful of sauce, a slice of ham, another of cheese, and broil in a hot oven until the cheese melts.
